Here’s a clean, no-nonsense **detailed summary of the Glendale Road Safety Enforcement Proposal** based on the uploaded presentation .

---

### Executive Purpose

The proposal positions **Traffic Owl** as a turnkey traffic-enforcement partner for the City of **Glendale**, targeting speeding, red-light running, and intersection-related violations. The stated goal is to materially reduce serious crashes and fatalities while aligning Glendale with **California’s Vision Zero** and **AB 645** automated enforcement pilot.

---

### The Problem Being Addressed

Glendale experiences **dozens of serious traffic crashes every year**, with fatalities occurring at intersections due primarily to speeding and red-light violations. This places the city out of step with Vision Zero objectives and exposes it to growing safety, legal, and reputational risk—especially ahead of regional events like the **2028 Los Angeles Olympics**.

---

### Proposed Solution: Traffic Owl Platform

The solution is built around two core components:

**1. Falcon Enforcement Systems**
Falcon is a **multi-violation, automated enforcement unit** capable of operating:

* Stationary
* Mobile
* Portable

It is designed for **24/7 unattended operation** and supports simultaneous detection of multiple violation types.

**Key technical differentiator**:
Falcon uses **dual verification** for speed enforcement:

* Radar measurement
* Video frame-by-frame analysis
  Both operate at the same time, improving accuracy and evidentiary robustness compared to single-method systems.

**2. Owl Grid (Central Platform)**
A unified monitoring and analytics platform that:

* Connects all Falcon units
* Centralizes enforcement data
* Provides traffic flow analytics
* Enables scalable citywide deployment

All equipment is described as **locally manufactured and serviced in California**, which is positioned as a procurement and reliability advantage.

---

### System Architecture & Hardware

Falcon systems are **modular**, allowing flexible configurations depending on site needs. A deployment may include:

* Multiple measuring modules (Types 1–4)
* Video modules (Types 1–3)
* Radar modules
* External server modules
* Additional peripheral equipment

This modularity allows Glendale to tailor enforcement by intersection, corridor, or zone rather than deploying a one-size-fits-all camera.

---

### Functional Capabilities

Falcon systems provide:

* Automated **ALPR (license plate recognition)**
* Continuous traffic flow monitoring
* Statistical traffic analysis
* Automated violation capture and documentation

**Violation coverage is unusually broad**, including:

* Speeding
* Red-light violations
* Illegal turns and lane misuse
* Failure to yield (including pedestrian right-of-way)
* Oncoming traffic and overtaking violations
* Parking and stopping violations
* Railroad crossing violations
* Unauthorized lights/sirens
* Residential safety compliance
* Insurance and inspection enforcement

This breadth is a central competitive claim.

---

### Pilot & Deployment Plan

**Stage 1**

* Install **5 Falcon camera complexes**
* Initial site: Brand Blvd & Colorado St

**Stage 2**

* Expand to **18 total intersections**
* Selection based on collision and safety data

**Timeline**

* **6–9 months** for full deployment

The plan emphasizes incremental rollout rather than a risky citywide “big bang.”

---

### Financial Framework

* **Cost per camera complex**: ~$5,000
* **Revenue assumptions**:

    * ~$100 per citation
    * ~30 citations per day per unit
    * Estimated **annual gross revenue ≈ $21M**

The model is presented as a **transparent flat-fee structure**, positioning Traffic Owl as aligned with city incentives rather than revenue-sharing opacity.

---

### Strategic Context: 2028 Olympics

The proposal explicitly ties the platform to **Olympic-scale traffic pressure**:

* Millions of visitors
* Global scrutiny
* High demand on intersections near venues and corridors

Falcon + Owl Grid are framed as a way to ensure:

* Safer intersections
* Predictable traffic flow
* A positive international image for Los Angeles and surrounding cities like Glendale

---

### Competitive Positioning

Compared against **Verra Mobility, RedSpeed, and Sensys Gatso**, Falcon claims advantages in:

**Technology**

* Dual radar + video speed verification (competitors typically use a single method)

**Flexibility**

* Fixed, mobile, and portable deployment options
* Comparable to Gatso, more flexible than RedSpeed

**Coverage**

* Broadest violation set among competitors

**Smart City Integration**

* Infrared and thermal imaging
* RFID support
* Structural monitoring (bridges, roads)
* Environmental sensors
* Cloud-based analytics

Competitors are largely positioned as **enforcement-only**, whereas Falcon is positioned as **enforcement + smart-city infrastructure**.

---

### Bottom-Line Message

* **Falcon’s strengths**: accuracy via dual detection, wide violation coverage, modular deployment, smart-city extensibility, and a unified Owl Grid platform.
* **Competitors’ strengths**: longer U.S. operating history and certifications (especially Verra Mobility).
* **Recommended sales strategy**: lead with an all-inclusive per-camera package and upsell advanced monitoring modules later.

---

### Conclusion

The proposal frames Glendale as a **showcase city** for California’s automated enforcement future. Traffic Owl positions Falcon and Owl Grid as delivering:

* Improved public safety
* Significant municipal revenue
* Regional leadership in Vision Zero compliance

This is not pitched as “just cameras,” but as **foundational urban enforcement infrastructure**.
